
📺 **Project Overview of the Case Study**

BrightTV is a media broadcasting company where television viewers subscribe to stream and
to watch various scheduled channel contents. The primary objective is to rapidly expand
the platform's active subscription base and optimize scheduled programming. This project
provides an end-to-end data analytics solution that evaluates historical viewership data
to uncover trends, improve viewer preservation, and to maximize advertising revenue slots

**How I approached the the Case Study and the Insights I found**

The main objective was to Identify insights that will help BrightTV increase its subscriber base
during the current financial year. I therefore used the follwowing approaches:

- **Step 1: Understanding the data**

I first reviewed the available tables and fields, including:

- Customer demographics (age, gender, location)
- Subscription details
- Viewing transactions
- Content watched
- Viewing frequency
- Subscription status
- Devices used
Payment history

The aim was to understand what each field represented and to identify missing or inconsistent data.

- **Step 2: Cleaning the data**

I checked for:

- Duplicated field of customers
- Missing values
- Invalid dates
- Incorrect subscription records
- Inconsistent customer IDs

Cleaning ensures the analysis is reliable.

- **Step 3: Explore customer behaviour**

I calculated key metrics such as:

- Total subscribers
- Active vs inactive subscribers
- New subscriptions
- Viewing hours
- Average viewing sessions
- Popular content categories
- Peak viewing times
- Customer preservation 

- **Step 4: Segment customers**

Customers were grouped according to behaviour, for example:

- Frequent viewers
- Casual viewers
- New subscribers
- High-value subscribers

Segmentation helps CVM target each group differently.

- **Step 5: Identifying drivers of subscriptions**

I looked for relationships between:

- Viewing frequency and renewal
- Content preferences and subscription duration
- Age groups and viewing habits
- Devices and engagement
- Regions with high or low growth

- **Step 6: Made recommendations**

Finally, I translated the analysis into business recommendations that could implemented.
